Marie Thérèse

Mother Marie-Thérèse de Lamourous (1754–1836) was a remarkable French laywoman and a devoted Catholic who played a significant role during and after the French Revolution. Working closely with Blessed William Joseph Chaminade, she became a key figure in the Marianist movement. Deeply committed to social justice and rehabilitation, she founded the Misericorde of Bordeaux, a refuge and reform house for women seeking to leave lives of prostitution and find dignity and spiritual renewal. Known for her compassion, strength, and unwavering faith, Mother Marie-Thérèse left a lasting legacy in the Catholic Church through her leadership, charitable work, and dedication to helping marginalized women reintegrate into society with hope and purpose.
